# Code of Conduct

GuanZai welcomes people across languages, cultures, identities, experience levels, and disciplines. We want a community where technical precision and human dignity reinforce each other.

## Our standards

We expect participants to:

- discuss ideas and evidence without diminishing people;
- give specific, constructive feedback and accept correction with grace;
- respect privacy, consent, attribution, and differing constraints;
- avoid harassment, threats, discrimination, sexualized attention, doxxing, and sustained disruption;
- step back when a conversation becomes heated and help restore a workable space.

These standards apply in project repositories, discussions, events, and other spaces where someone represents the project.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may edit or remove contributions and may warn, temporarily restrict, or permanently ban participants whose behavior harms the community. Decisions should be proportionate, documented privately when appropriate, and protect the privacy of reporters.

To report conduct concerns, use the repository's private contact method when one is listed. If none is available, open a public issue requesting a private maintainer channel without including names, screenshots, or sensitive details. Security vulnerabilities should follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

Maintainers must not retaliate against good-faith reports. Anyone with a conflict of interest should recuse themselves from handling the report.
